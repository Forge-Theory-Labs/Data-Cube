The speed of light in a vacuum is exactly 299,792,458 metres per second
Light takes approximately 8 minutes and 20 seconds to travel from the Sun to Earth
The visible spectrum of light ranges from approximately 380nm to 700nm wavelength
A photon has zero rest mass but carries momentum and energy
Light behaves as both a wave and a particle depending on how it is observed
Total internal reflection occurs when light hits a boundary at an angle greater than the critical angle

The human brain contains approximately 86 billion neurons
Neurons communicate via electrochemical signals called action potentials
The brain consumes roughly 20% of the body's total energy despite being only 2% of its mass
Sleep is essential for memory consolidation and synaptic pruning
The hippocampus plays a central role in forming new long-term memories
Neuroplasticity allows the brain to rewire itself in response to experience

Water is the only natural substance found in all three states at Earth's surface temperatures
A water molecule consists of two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom
Water has an unusually high specific heat capacity of 4182 J/kg·K
Ice is less dense than liquid water which is why it floats
Water is a universal solvent due to its polar molecular structure
The surface tension of water allows insects like water striders to walk on it

The Milky Way galaxy contains between 100 and 400 billion stars
It takes the Solar System approximately 225 million years to orbit the galactic centre
The nearest star to Earth beyond the Sun is Proxima Centauri at 4.24 light-years away
Black holes are regions where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape
A neutron star is so dense that a teaspoon of its material would weigh around a billion tonnes
The observable universe is estimated to be 93 billion light-years in diameter

DNA is a double helix structure composed of four nucleotide bases: adenine, thymine, cytosine and guanine
The human genome contains approximately 3.2 billion base pairs
Only about 1.5% of the human genome codes for proteins
DNA replication occurs at a rate of roughly 1000 base pairs per second in humans
Mutations in the BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes significantly increase the risk of breast and ovarian cancer
CRISPR-Cas9 allows precise editing of DNA sequences using a guide RNA and a cutting enzyme

The first programmable electronic computer, ENIAC, was completed in 1945
Moore's Law observed that transistor counts on chips roughly double every two years
The Internet originated from ARPANET, a US Defense Department project launched in 1969
A qubit can exist in a superposition of 0 and 1 simultaneously unlike a classical bit
The first commercial smartphone with a touchscreen was the Apple iPhone released in 2007
Quantum entanglement allows two particles to be correlated regardless of the distance between them
